WELCOME TO BATANES

Escape to Batanes: Where Untamed Beauty Meets Enduring Culture Journey to the breathtaking archipelago of Batanes, the Philippines' northernmost frontier, and discover a landscape sculpted by wind and time. Imagine emerald rolling hills dotted with grazing cattle, dramatic coastlines pounded by turquoise waves, and charming stone houses nestled amidst rolling green pastures. Batanes is more than just a visual feast; it's a tapestry woven with the rich cultural heritage of the resilient Ivatan people. Immerse yourself in nature's grandeur: Hike through rolling hills carpeted with vibrant wildflowers and encounter the iconic stone houses, marvels of traditional Ivatan architecture. Stand in awe at the dramatic coastline, where towering cliffs meet the vastness of the Pacific Ocean. Witness the raw power of nature as waves crash against the rocks, creating a symphony of sound and spray. Explore hidden coves and pristine beaches, perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and soaking up the tranquility of this remote paradise. Embrace the warmth of Ivatan culture: Delve into the rich history and traditions of the Ivatan people, known for their resilience and respect for nature. Visit traditional villages and witness the unique way of life, where time seems to slow down and simplicity reigns supreme. Sample the distinct flavors of Ivatan cuisine, featuring fresh seafood, locally grown vegetables, and unique spices, offering a taste of the island's bounty. Beyond the natural beauty and cultural immersion, Batanes offers a diverse range of activities: Embark on an exhilarating trek to the iconic lighthouse at Cape Engaño, offering panoramic views of the island and the vast ocean beyond. Spot playful dolphins frolicking in the crystal-clear waters or try your hand at kayaking or stand-up paddleboarding for a unique perspective of the coastline. Learn the art of traditional Ivatan weaving and take home a unique souvenir handcrafted by local artisans. Batanes is an escape unlike any other. It's a place to reconnect with nature, immerse yourself in a vibrant culture, and create memories that will last a lifetime. Are you ready to embark on this unforgettable adventure?

BATANES' DELICACIES

UVED

Called ‘tabtab‘ in Sabtang (one of the 3 inhabited islands in Batanes). It’s basically made of finely-grated corn (which is found at the base of a trunk of a banana plant) that’s mixed with ground meat (beef or pork), and minced fish. Afterward, it is seasoned with onion, garlic, salt, and pepper. This will be rolled into balls or served as-is and can either be with or without its broth.

DIBANG

A common fish that you will find in the islands is dibang or flying fish — yes, it does ‘fly’ or leap out of the water, a defense mechanism to evade predators. To date, it has different variations.
