BANAUE RICE TERRACES

IFUGAO, CORDILLERA REGION

WELCOME TO BANAUE RICE TERRACES

Carved into the verdant slopes of the Philippine Cordilleras, the Banaue Rice Terraces are a breathtaking testament to human ingenuity and perseverance. These cascading emerald steps, sculpted by the Ifugao people over 2,000 years ago, are not just agricultural marvels, but also a living cultural landscape recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Imagine yourself standing at a breathtaking viewpoint, gazing upon a seemingly endless tapestry of rice terraces cascading down the mountainsides. The emerald hues shift and shimmer with the changing light, creating a scene of unparalleled beauty. As you breathe in the cool mountain air and feel the gentle breeze caress your face, you'll be transported to a world of serenity and wonder.

Your journey to Banaue is more than just a sightseeing expedition; it's an opportunity to immerse yourself in the rich cultural heritage of the Ifugao people. Engage with the locals, learn about their traditional farming practices and way of life, and witness their intricate weaving techniques. Savor their delicious cuisine, prepared with fresh, locally-sourced ingredients, and discover the warmth and hospitality that defines their culture.

Beyond the cultural immersion, adventure awaits in Banaue. Embark on exhilarating treks through verdant valleys, following ancient trails that weave through the heart of the rice terraces. Hike to vantage points like Hango Rice Terraces or Batad Rice Terraces, where panoramic views will leave you breathless. Explore hidden waterfalls cascading down moss-covered cliffs, and discover ancient burial caves that whisper tales of the past.

The Banaue Rice Terraces are more than just a breathtaking landscape; they are a symbol of resilience, ingenuity, and the harmonious relationship between humanity and nature. A visit here is an unforgettable experience that will leave you awestruck by the sheer beauty of the surroundings and the rich cultural tapestry woven into its very fabric. So, come, embark on this unforgettable journey and discover the magic that awaits you in the heart of the Banaue Rice Terraces.

ONE OF THE IFUGAO'S FOLK SONG

In Maliyah Hi Bale Yu

click the play button
"In Maliyah Hi Bale Yu" is the title of a popular Ifugao folk song from the Philippines. It translates to "I came to your house" in English.

BANAUE'S DELICACIES

BINAKLE

Kiniwar is a sweet sticky rice dessert that is popular in Benguet, a province in the Cordillera region of the Philippines. This indigenous delicacy is made from glutinous rice that is pounded and molded into small cakes or balls using a traditional wooden mortar and pestle. The rice cakes are then cooked in coconut milk and sugar, resulting in a soft and chewy treat that melts in your mouth. Kiniwar is often served during festivals and special occasions, as a symbol of gratitude and celebration
